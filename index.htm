Our Beers: Currently Brewing

In preparation for our first public pour at Beers of the Burgh, we have hand-picked a smaller group of recipes from our larger catalogue that highlight our varied brewing styles. Check back in the future for a comprehensive listing of our beers and open sourced recipes, but for now takes a look at our festival line-up.

  • EQIPA v1.1

    Equinox IPA

    An American IPA brewed almost exclusively with Hop Breeding Company's newest hop Equinox™. Expect a rush of melon, berry, and citrus-Lime characteristics with a beautiful mouth feel from the high oil content of Equinox™.

    Recipe Specifics

  • M3IPA v1.1

    Mango Triple IPA

    Seven different hops combine for a melon mango tropical punch to the mouth. The real mango helps to hide the high alcohol and bitterness while playing right along with the hops. The key to this style in our opinion is to keep the final gravity low as not to tread near a barley wine style wise.

    Recipe Specifics

  • DAB v1.0

    Dry-hopped American Blonde Ale

    Clocking in at 3.2% ABV this is a truly sessionable beer but don't be fooled... the flavor and body are not lacking because of it. A solid malt profile is supported by a delicate Mosaic™ and Centennial dry hop.

    Recipe Specifics

  • BDS v1.1

    Belgian Dark Strong Ale

    Dried fruit, caramel, ethanol, spice, phenols... this is a complex beer.

    Recipe Specifics

  • RW v1.1

    Rye Wine

    An American style barley wine with 35% of the grist made up of rye malts. Strong rye and caramel notes are balanced by a firm bitterness. One of first beers ticketed for some barrel aging.

About Abjuration Brewing

Abjuration Brewing consists of four longtime friends and brewers from the Pittsburgh Area who are currently ramping up to open a nano brewery on a 1-3 barrel level. Our style is diverse, but we lean towards hoppy, clean American ales.

Local & Nano

We aren't trying to take over the world, just trying to make delicious hand-crafted beers for our neighbors in Pittsburgh, PA. The freshest ingredients come from your own backyard, so we attempt to locally source ingredients when possible.

Open Source

A good beer tastes better when shared with friends, and we feel the same way about a good recipe. Here at Abjuration we plan on posting all of our recipies and variations as free for public use. If you love something, set it free.

Experimental

We approach brewing with a mad scientist ideology. We use new and experimental yeast, malt, adjuncts and whatever else we can get our hands on. Small scale brewing allows for creative brewing.
